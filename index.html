About Me

My name is Tim Fonshell,
Engineer B.Sc.

Software development has been an early passion of mine. I always saw it as a tool to create something amazing out of thin air. Even though software development as just a part of my studies of product development mechatronics, I started my career as an embedded software developer. Working in the automotive industry I gained a massive amount of experience, not only in software but also in the hardware it controls.

After a few years, I decided to leave the automotive field and become a classical software developer. I again chose a steep learning curve and am now developing desktop applications for Windows and Android applications.

Next to my projects as an employee, I keep learning new topics and developing my own projects, too. Something that stuck with me from my studies is 3D printing. I still possess professional CAD skills that I use to design parts that I then print.

Software-developer at Grauthoff Turengruppe GmbH

Together with my colleagues I keep improving and developing our calculation and pricing software for our product portfolio. I programm mostly in C#, using Visual Studio. Additionaly I write small tools in PHP, or PowerShell to automate reoccuring processes.
Besides the software development I am also a direct contact for our customers if the encounter any problems with our software. I then debug the software according to their problem reports and deploy fixes.

Application Engineer at BHTC GmbH

At BHTC I developed embedded software for automotive ECUs with Matlab Simulink an C++ under very strict quality requirements. One of my task was to maintain the customer requirements check them for feasability in close contact with the customer. I was also on site, working with the customer on prototypes checking and settings up our software.

Mar. 2014 - Dec. 2018

Application Engineer at dSPACE GmbH

At dSPACE my responsibility was planning and programming hardware in the loop simulators for automotive tests. Our main development tool was Matlad Simulink and for some special cases I had to programm an application on an FPGA.
After finishing the simulators I ad to commission them together with the customers and also train the customer in the operation of his new tool.
I was also a technical guide for our sales team.

Jul. 2016 - Dec. 2018

After a few years as an application engineer at dSPACE I decided to extend my responsibilities and became a product trainer for the SCALEXIO system. It was my responsibility to maintain the training material, keep it up to date and teach our customers the proper operation of the system.
